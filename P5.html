Teaching children programming via Lego robotics

ROLE

Teaching aged from 5 to 7 students in a private institute

SOFTWARE

Lego education Essential application
(Icon/Word block coding)

HARDWARE

Lego education Essential brick set

A pattern drawing robot

In this class, students assembled robots that could draw fixed patterns. After building the robots, students designed their own patterns with their hands and wrote the codes for their designs.

A pickup truck robot

In this class, students assembled their own pickup trucks to enable people to sit based on the Lego assembly instructions. Before they wrote the code, they set their own paths to visit on the map. Once the students designed their paths, they wrote the code to make their robots follow their paths. 

A hockey robot

In this class, students played hockey games against the robot goalkeeper. Students used their hockey sticks to kick the red ball around while avoiding various obstacles. If the students' ball passed the goal line, then by using the color sensor behind the goal line, they detected the ball's color, whether it had passed the goal line, and if so, a computer played the sound of applause.

A maze robot

Students in this class set multiple obstacles, including start and goal-in points on the Lego. While the Lego plate is being moved to make the ball go onto the goal-in point, a gyro sensor on the robot counts the number of movements. When the robot counted the movement, students programmed the computer to make the sound of a laser and also display colors on the Lego light brick. After students completed the mission, they checked the recorded numbers of their movements on the computer.

A path recording robot

In this class, students built robots that ran on the map. Students designed their paths for the robots on the map and checked how many "go forward/ go backward/ turn left/ turn right" commands were used as a bar graph.

A golf robot

In this class, students made robots that mimicked golf swings. By setting the goal-in location far away, adjusting the power of swinging, and adjusting the height of swinging, students learned how to make the ball go far away.

Is block-based programming language helpful for adults when learning general programming languages?

Purpose of the project

Since the light was shed on computer science, universities in Korea have tried to encourage more students to study interdisciplinary fields. As a part of that attempt, the school started a new policy requiring that first-year students take a course in learning a programming language. Since non-computer science majors do not need to understand programming thoroughly, the school decided to utilize a block-based programming language to teach non-CS majors. To investigate its efficacy for non-CS significant students, we took part in the class as assistants for the class and conducted several tests and satisfaction surveys for students.

Education contents in the class

Loop

Parameter

Hardware programming

Result of the project

Students improved their conceptual understanding of programming by using block-based programming languages. However, the effect of this approach was limited. Non-Computer Science majors performed particularly poorly in Python examinations. Furthermore, the poor performance in the test may be caused by the hostile attitudes of non-CS students. Since they took the programming class by force of the school's policy, they showed very unsatisfactory attitudes in the survey toward this class.

Translated abstract
Since universities have recognized the importance of Computer Science (CS), CS education is being provided to all students regardless of their majors. Although the type of language used varies depending on the goals of each university, block-based programming languages are used primarily in programming education for beginners. In this study, we found how effective block-based programming languages are in general-purpose programming language education. After teaching the concept of computer science through a block-based language, we re-taught the same students the same contents in Python. We conducted achievement evaluations and surveys to measure the effectiveness of block-based language education. As a result, a block-based programming language helped students enhance their conceptual understanding of programming. However, it was confirmed that educating general-purpose programming languages (e.g., Python and C++) based on block-based language experiences had a limitation.

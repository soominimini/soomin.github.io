Understanding Human Empathy Toward Robots

Exploring emotional attribution and helping behaviors toward socially expressive robots

This project investigates how humans respond empathetically to social robots in emotionally charged scenarios. Using the Vector robot, we designed an experiment where the robot appeared to struggle or request help, and observed how participants reacted.

The study draws on theories of moral psychology and human social cognition to evaluate whether participants would express concern, intervene to help, or mirror emotional responses—behaviors typically reserved for human social agents.

Our preliminary findings show that participants often **attributed emotions and intentions** to the robot and responded in kind, even in the absence of human-like appearance. These results suggest that **robot behavior alone can trigger empathetic concern**, especially when accompanied by expressive movement, tone, and framing.

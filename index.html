About

I’m Soomin Shin, a Ph.D. candidate in Electrical and Computer Engineering at the University of Waterloo, advised by Professor Kerstin Dautenhahn. My research bridges Human-Robot Interaction (HRI), socially assistive robotics, and large language models (LLMs) to create intelligent, user-centered systems for real-world applications in therapy and education.

At the Social and Intelligent Robotics Research Lab, I lead projects on AI-driven robotic systems and co-designed therapy games, collaborating closely with therapists to ensure clinical usability and adaptability.

Prior to my Ph.D., I conducted research on contextual affect modeling at Korea University, comparing human emotion perception with CNN-based models—work that was presented at ACM ICMI. I’m passionate about building AI systems that are not only intelligent, but also empathetic and collaborative.

Current Research Projects

  • LLM-driven Therapy Robot System – Developing a customizable robot system using large language models and RAG to support therapist-driven adaptations in real time. (Ongoing)
  • Co-designed Speech Therapy Games – Robot-integrated therapeutic approach built in collaboration with SLPs. Presented at ICSR 2024.
  • Multi-Agent Robot Trust Studies – Exploring how perceived robot hierarchies affect human trust in medical settings. Published at IROS 2023.
  • Contextual Affect Recognition – Comparing human vs. CNN emotion recognition models under contextual conditions. Presented at ACM ICMI 2022.

Previous Projects

  • Empathy Toward Robots – Behavioral study using Vector robot to explore how people show empathy toward robotic agents.
